Enforcement of arbitral awards between China and Hong Kong
By Jonathan Rostron, of Sinclair Roche & Temperley, Hong Kong
The 1958 New York Convention1 provides for the reciprocal enforcement of arbitration awards. A central feature of the Convention is that the awards be international in nature. One of the concerns of lawyers involved in trade between Hong Kong and the Mainland has been directed to the enforceability of Hong Kong arbitration awards in China after the hand-over (1st July 1997). Until now there has been a void.

In mid-June 1999 a consensus was reached between the government of Hong Kong Special Administrative Region (SAR) and Chinese authorities for an arrangement for the reciprocal enforcement of arbitral awards between the two jurisdictions.
There have been concerns over the enforceability of Hong Kong arbitration awards in China after the hand-over.
The convention continued to apply to Hong Kong after the hand-over. The enforcement arrangements with other jurisdictions remains unchanged except for that with Mainland China since the convention, being an international agreement, no longer applied to the enforcement of awards between the Mainland and Hong Kong. A new arrangement has been reached, made in accordance with the spirit of the New York Convention, which will allow awards made by over 100 Mainland Chinese arbitral authorities to be enforced in the Hong Kong SAR when the Arbitration (Amendment) Bill is introduced into the Legislative Council (and passed). The Chinese Courts in the Mainland also agree to enforce the awards made in Hong Kong pursuant to the Arbitration Ordnance of Hong Kong SAR. The new arrangement will also apply to arbitral awards made by the Mainland or Hong Kong SAR published after 1st July 1997.

Brief details of the arrangement have been published in Hong Kong which will be clarified further when the bill does go through government channels in Hong Kong. There is also a two-way public policy exception which permits the courts in Hong Kong or Mainland China to decline to enforce the award if contrary to the public policy of each jurisdiction.

Parties which attempted in vain to enforce awards during the hiatus between 1st July 1997 and the coming into force of the new arrangement will be allowed to make a fresh application for enforcement.

1 Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards, 1958. See article "The Enforcement of Arbitration Awards - The New York Convention, 1958" in Gard News issue No. 144.
